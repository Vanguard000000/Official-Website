# Resume — you need one

Kushagra and Shreyas both have resumes in their repos and got feedback on them today. You don't have one yet — this folder is where it goes.

## The blueprint (one page, in this order)

1. **Header** — name, "League City, TX" (city/state only — never your street address in a public repo), email, **link to this website**, link to your GitHub.
2. **Education** — school, expected graduation year, GPA (label weighted vs unweighted correctly).
3. **Experience / Activities** — you already have the material: FRC robotics, your activities and volunteering pages on this site are basically a draft. Each item gets 1–3 bullets about what *you did*, with numbers where they exist (hours, placements, team results).
4. **Honors & Awards** — newest first, high school only unless something older is exceptional.
5. **Skills** — only things you'd be comfortable being asked about. Building this website with AI-assisted workflows belongs here; spell every tool name correctly.

## Rules learned from your classmates' feedback today

- **One page.** Theirs were three; both are cutting down.
- No date of birth, no street address — this repo is public.
- Every date consistent with one as-of date.
- Say each thing exactly once.
- Your strongest material goes where it's seen, not buried at the bottom.

## How to build it

Write the content yourself first — bullet points in plain text, your words. Then have your agent help with formatting. Same rule as the journal: an agent can shape it, but a resume in someone else's voice reads like one.

When a draft exists, ask for a critique and you'll get the same treatment they did.
