Research Projects

🧪

SEFH — Effects of Artificial Sweeteners on Gut Microbiome

In 7th grade, I qualified for SEFH (Science and Engineering Fair of Houston) by conducting an independent research project on how common sweeteners affect Lactobacillus, a key genus in the human gut microbiome.

I tested sucrose, aspartame, fructose, and other artificial sweeteners to measure their impact on bacterial growth. The results showed that aspartame was particularly adverse to the gut microbiome, inhibiting Lactobacillus growth significantly compared to natural sugars.

This project sparked my interest in the intersection of biology, chemistry, and data analysis — skills I now apply to engineering problems.
